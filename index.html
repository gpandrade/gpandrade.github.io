About Me

I'm a second year Ph.D. student in computer science at CU Boulder. My research interests are in algorithmic economics, complex adaptive systems, local-to-global theory, and distributed multi-agent systems. I'm part of the CS Theory and Complex Systems groups here at CU and my advisors are Professors Rafael Frongillo and Joshua Grochow. Before coming here, I completed an M.S. in applied math and worked with Professor Robert Kozma as part of the Biologically Inspired Neural and Dynamical Systems Lab at UMass Amherst. During that time, I also worked as a graduate IT assistant in the department of mathematics & statistics. My undergraduate degree is also from UMass, where I majored in pure math with a minor in philosophy.
